1.What is MED-AI-CAL?
The webapp is designed to help elderly individuals manage their medications, monitor vital health data, and alert caregivers or family members if any health irregularities are detected. It’s especially helpful for those who live alone, promoting independence while ensuring safety and health monitoring.

2. How does the webapp remind users to take their medications?
The webapp sends timely notifications and reminders to the user’s mobile device, indicating when it's time to take each medication. Users can also customize these reminders based on their medication schedules to ensure they don’t miss any doses.

3. What health data does the webapp track?
The webapp tracks key vital signs such as heart rate, blood pressure, blood oxygen levels, and activity levels. This data is gathered through integration with compatible wearable devices, making it easy to monitor health trends over time.

4. How does the webapp notify caregivers or family members?
If irregularities in the user’s health data are detected (such as sudden spikes in heart rate or blood pressure), the webapp sends an alert to designated caregivers or family members. This allows them to take timely action if any health concerns arise.

5. Can the webapp integrate with wearable devices?
Yes, the webapp is compatible with various wearable devices that track health metrics. By connecting with wearables, the webapp can collect real-time data and provide more comprehensive monitoring and alerts.

6. What happens if the user misses a medication dose?
If a medication dose is missed, the webapp sends a follow-up reminder to the user. Additionally, caregivers or family members can receive a notification about the missed dose, allowing them to check in with the user if necessary.

7. Is the webapp easy to use for elderly users who may not be tech-savvy?
Absolutely. The webapp is designed with a user-friendly, intuitive interface, with large icons and simple navigation. Clear instructions and minimal steps help make it accessible even for users who may not be comfortable with technology.

8. How does the webapp ensure data privacy and security?
The webapp follows strict data privacy guidelines, using encryption to protect health data and ensuring only authorized users (like caregivers and family members) have access to the user’s information.

9. Can the webapp provide insights on the user’s health trends?
Yes, the webapp analyzes the collected health data over time to identify patterns and provide insights into the user’s health trends. This can help both users and caregivers detect any long-term changes or issues that may require medical attention.

10. Does the webapp work offline?
Basic features, such as medication reminders, work offline. However, data tracking, updates, and alert notifications require an internet connection to send data to caregivers and sync with wearable devices.

11. Can family members customize the type and frequency of alerts?
Yes, caregivers and family members can customize alert settings based on the user’s needs. This includes setting thresholds for vital signs, choosing notification preferences, and adjusting the frequency of reminders.

12. Is it possible to add multiple caregivers to receive notifications?
Yes, the webapp supports adding multiple caregivers, such as family members, friends, or healthcare providers, who can receive alerts and monitor the user’s health data.

13. Can the webapp help manage complex medication schedules?
Yes, the webapp allows users to input detailed medication schedules, including different dosages and times for each medication. It can handle complex medication routines and adjust reminders accordingly.

14. What if the user needs emergency help?
The webapp can include an emergency SOS button, allowing users to quickly notify their caregivers or emergency contacts if they need immediate assistance. Some versions may also connect directly to emergency services.
